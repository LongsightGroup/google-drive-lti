Introduction

GDrive provides an easy way to share Google Drive folders, subfolders, documents, drawings and other Google items with all members of a CTools site. GDrive automatically accesses the roster of students or participant list in your site.

Please note:

  • Only instructors and project owners can share one of their folders.
  • For each site, only one folder from Google Drive can be shared, but that folder can contain an unlimited number of sub folders.
  • At this time, GDrive will not work in classes with more than 200 students.

Terms used in this help document:

  • "Google Drive" refers to your Google Drive.
  • "GDrive" is the Google Drive tool that you can add to a site such as an LMS site.

Supported Browsers

Most modern browsers are supported, except:

  • IE 8 and earlier are not supported.
  • Firefox is partially supported -- scrolling back to display prompts doesn't work in some cases.

Sharing a Google Drive folder with your site's GDrive

When you share a folder that exists on your Google Drive with GDrive, the folder will become the top-level folder in GDrive. You only need to do this one time for each site. You can add subfolders to this folder.

To share a Google Drive folder with GDrive on your site:

  1. While you are in your site, click GDrive in the left-hand menubar.

    All the folders on your Google Drive will automatically appear in the site's GDrive window. (It may take a little while for them to load.)

  2. Click the Share Folder button to the right of the folder you would like to link to and use as the top-level folder.
  3. An email preference box will open asking if you would like to send email notifications to all members of your site.

    Once you have completed these steps, you will see the shared folder in both the GDrive window and on your Google Drive.

    Viewing a folder or subfolder from your site on your Google Drive

    1. While in GDrive, click the folder's name in the list.
    2. In the window that opens, click Open in Drive in the upper right-hand corner.

    The open folder will appear in a Google Drive window.

    Changing the top-level folder

    You can create a new top-level folder in your site's GDrive or share a different folder from your Google Drive. You will first need to unshare the original folder.

    1. While in your site, click Unshare to the right of the folder you no longer want to use as the top-level folder.
    2. To link to a folder in your Google Drive, click Share Folder to the right of the folder you want to link to and use as the top folder. Type a name, and choose your email preference.
    3. To create a new folder, click the Create & Share Folder button.

      (You may need to refresh your screen for the button to appear.)

      Type a name for the new folder in the small window that opens. Then choose your email preference.

    You will see the folder in both the GDrive window and on your Google Drive.

    Folders and items in folders are listed alphabetically.

    Adding a subfolder

    1. In the Add dropdown menu to the right of the folder to which you would like to add a subfolder, select Add Folder.
    2. Name the folder.
    3. Click OK.

    Adding documents, spreadsheets, presentations, and drawings

    1. In the Add dropdown menu next to the folder to which you would like to add an item, select the type.
    2. Name the item.
    3. Click OK.

    Deleting folders and other items

    1. Click the Delete button to the right of the item you wish to delete.
    2. Click OK to confirm.

    Note: This cannot be undone. It will also delete the item in Google Drive. Deleting a folder will also delete its contents. (The top-level folder cannot be deleted -- only unshared.)

    In GDrive, users with "Can Edit" permissions can delete a document they do not own. In Google Drive, they can remove the document to move it to Trash.

    Common questions about sharing and permissions in GDrive

    If I unshare a folder in GDrive, does that remove site member permissions for the folder on Google Drive?

    Yes. All site members (with the exception of the owner of the folder) will have their permissions removed.

    If I remove a user from my site roster, will his or her permissions be removed from any shared folders?

    No. You must remove individual users you wish to no longer have access to shared Google folders (or choose to unshare it with the whole site).

    If I delete a shared folder, will users still have access?

    When you delete a Google folder, it first goes to the Trash where it can still be restored. When you empty the trash, the folder is permanently deleted.

    Users cannot access folders in the Trash or those that have been permanently deleted. However, if a folder in the Trash is restored, user permissions will also be restored.

    If I share a folder between two sites (A & B) and unshare a folder on Site B, will I retain access to the folder on Site A?

    Yes, you can still access the folder on Site A. However, to continue to have access to the folder on Google Drive, you must first go to Site A and open the folder from there. You only need to do this once. After that, you will always be able open the folder while in Google Drive."

    What folders can I share?

    You can share folders that have either "Can Edit" or "Owner" permissions.

    Who can delete a document on a site?

    In GDrive, users with "Can Edit" permissions can delete a document they do not own. In Google Drive, they can remove the document to move it to Trash. An example of this workflow is multiple instructors on a site can edit and remove documents.

    Known Issues

    • Search results only match against the start of words. For example, if there is a folder named "my testfest" and "fest" is the search term, the folder will not be returned. If "test" is used, it will.

      Workaround: To see the folder contents that do not match the search text, the search field needs to be cleared.


    • Expanding search results does not display subfolders unless the subfolders match the search results.
